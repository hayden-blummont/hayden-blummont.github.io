How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Seeking happiness in this context through 'pleasure' means going after highs. It's instinctive to do this but it's super unsustainable and often has a big low swing afterwards.
Passion is when you're getting your happiness from being in 'flow' or being and feeling productive and engaged. This one is harder to get into but ultimately, the pay off tends to be better and there isn't such a low afterwards.
The 'higher purpose' kind of happiness derives from being part of something bigger than yourself that has meaning to you or allows you to feel meaningful. This is considered the most sustainable.
According to the author, we should spend the most time on the 'higher purpose' stuff, then on 'passion' and sometimes the 'pleasure' side.

Other musings

The section about learning optimism and unlearning pessimism is probably the one that has grabbed me the most so far. I found it particularly interesting when it's mentioned that it takes three good things to make up for one bad one. I've always been aware that I give much more weight to the negative things in my life than the positives and had wondered why I couldn't just be happy with the great things! It's nice to know that it's not uncommon and that it makes sense.
